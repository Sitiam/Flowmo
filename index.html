What is Flowtime?

Flowtime is a time management technique where a stopwatch is used to track the amount of time spent working. You work on a task for as long as you need to, stopping the timer and taking a break as soon as you feel as if you start to lose concentration or feel fatigued.

Break times generally last one-fifth of your working time. For example, if you work for 50 minutes, you should take a 10 minute break.


Why not Pomodoro?

One of the disadvantages of the pomodoro technique is that the timer consistently interrupts you, preventing you from fully immersing yourself into a task.

Flowtime, on the other hand, does not interrupt you during a focus session, allowing you to fully concentrate on the task at hand.
